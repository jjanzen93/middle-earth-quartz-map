People
[[Men]]
The Dúnedain are descendants of the Men of Westernesse (Númenor) who survived the downfall of their kingdom. Their king, Elendil, led them in [[The Last Alliance of Elves and Men]]. Additionally, their king granted the Shire to the Hobbits during [[The Founding of the Shire]]. Once Arnor fell into ruin, they became the wandering Rangers of the North. They now secretly guard the borders of peaceful places like the Shire and Bree-land.

They are unusually tall with dark hair, pale skin, and grey eyes. They live longer lives than ordinary men, and are the greatest trackers and hunters in Middle-earth. In other words, they shop at Hot Topic and have a very standard-fare D&D character tragic backstory.

**Citations:** _The Fellowship of the Ring_, Book 1, Ch. 10: Strider (p. 169-172); Book 2, Ch. 2: The Council of Elrond (p. 242-247).

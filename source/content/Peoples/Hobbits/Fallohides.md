People
There were fewer Fallohides than either of the other Hobbit branches. They originated furthest north and were the last to cross the mountains during [[The Wandering Days]]. However, it was two Fallohides, Marcho and Blanco, that led the mass migration leading to [[The Founding of the Shire]]. 

They had lighter skin and hair than other Hobbits, were taller and skinnier, and preferred the forest to the fields, hunting instead of farming for their food. In a similar way to the Stoors and Men, the Fallohides were more friendly with Elves. 

Fallohide ancestry has remained strong in important families, like the Tooks and Brandybucks, even after the Stoors and Harfoots came together in the Shire. They are less handy, but are far more musically and poetically inclined. Additionally, they tend to be more adventurous and bold. This is why they became leaders of Shire communities, and explains why big changes and historical events that involve the Shire are often set in motion by Fallohides like Marcho, Blanco, Pippin, Meriadoc, and Frodo.

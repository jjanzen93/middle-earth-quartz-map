People
The Stoors stayed by the Great River Anduin for a long time after the Harfoots had already left during [[The Wandering Days]], but some eventually moved west. The rest did as well some time later, mostly settling in the marshes region. Smeagol and his family were Stoors, which explains why he was fishing and swimming in the river when the One Ring was found.

The Stoors were broader and heavier than the other branches, with larger hands and feet. They preferred flat lands and riversides, and for a long time were the only Hobbits known to regularly use boats or swim. Nowadays, of course, those living on the Brandywine use boats in much the same fashion, but the stigma still persists in some other parts of the Shire.

**Citations:** _The Fellowship of the Ring_, Prologue: 1. Concerning Hobbits (p. 3-4); Book 1, Ch. 2: The Shadow of the Past (p. 52-54).

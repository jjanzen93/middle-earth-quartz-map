Event
The alliance was formed at the end of the Second Age in response to Sauron's growing influence in Mordor. It was led by Gil-galad of the [[High Elves]] and Elendil of the [[Dúnedain]] and [[Men of Gondor]]. The forces fought the Battle of Dagorlad in front of the Black Gate of Mordor, laying siege to the Dark Tower of Barad-dûm for seven years. [[Silvan Elves]] were also a part of the war, but they were largely independent from the Alliance, and this lack of true allies led to many losses for them. They won when Sauron's finger was cut off by Isildur and he lost control of the One Ring. Elendil and Gil-Galad were killed in the final battle, leaving both Elves and Men without their leaders.

The alliance failed in its purpose of destroying Sauron, as Isildur's refusal to destroy the One Ring only served to diminish him rather than be rid of him entirely.

**Citations:** _The Fellowship of the Ring_, Book 2, Ch. 2: The Council of Elrond (p. 242-246).

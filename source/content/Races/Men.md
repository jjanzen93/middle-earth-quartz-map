Race

Men participated in the great wars of the First and Second Ages, most notably in [[The Last Alliance of Elves and Men]]. During the Third Age, their kingdoms fractured, which has resulted in the rise of many subgroups of Men across Middle-earth, like the [[Dúnedain]], [[Men of Gondor]], and [[Rohirrim]].

It is hard to give a description of all Men when they are so varied and rarely described as a monolith compared to the other races. The [[Dúnedain]] are noble and highly skilled, with much longer lives than other Men. The [[Men of Gondor]] and the [[Rohirrim]] are certainly not humble, but are often rightfully proud and disciplined, as well as courageous. All Men are mortal, most are highly susceptible to the corruption of Rings of Power, but extremely adaptable and numerous. Their mortality is also seen as a good thing, as it entices them to be courageous and make their mark on the world in their short lives. Their tendency to make things happen means they will likely be the rulers of the earth when the Elves fade. 

Citations: _The Fellowship of the Ring_, Book 2, Ch. 2: The Council of Elrond (p. 242-247).
